When considering visible artifacts in a PDF, these are elements that are part of the visible content but are not the main textual or graphical content. These artifacts can include:

1. **Background Images or Colors**: Images or colors that appear behind the main content, often used for design or branding purposes.

2. **Watermarks**: Text or images that are overlaid on the document, often semi-transparent, to indicate the document's status (e.g., "Draft," "Confidential") or to prevent unauthorized copying.

3. **Headers and Footers**: Repeating elements at the top and bottom of each page, often containing page numbers, document titles, dates, or other metadata.

4. **Page Numbers**: Numbers that indicate the sequence of pages within the document.

5. **Borders and Rules**: Lines or boxes that frame the content or separate different sections of the document.

6. **Graphics and Icons**: Small images or symbols used to indicate specific information, such as bullet points, checkmarks, or other visual cues.

7. **Tables**: Structured layouts that organize data into rows and columns, often used for presenting tabular information.

8. **Lists**: Bulleted or numbered lists that organize information into a sequential or hierarchical format.

9. **Images**: Pictures, diagrams, or other graphical elements that are part of the visible content.

10. **Text Boxes**: Areas of text that are distinct from the main body text, often used for callouts, sidebars, or other supplementary information.

11. **Annotations**: Visible comments, highlights, or other markup added to the PDF, such as text comments, stamps, or shapes.

12. **Form Fields**: Interactive elements like text boxes, checkboxes, and radio buttons that are part of fillable forms.

13. **Hyperlinks**: Clickable text or images that direct the user to other parts of the document or to external web pages.

14. **Tables of Contents**: Lists of sections or chapters with page numbers, often at the beginning of the document, to help navigate the content.

15. **Graphs and Charts**: Visual representations of data, such as bar graphs, pie charts, or line graphs, that are part of the visible content.

These visible artifacts enhance the readability, organization, and visual appeal of the PDF document, making it easier for readers to understand and navigate the content.
